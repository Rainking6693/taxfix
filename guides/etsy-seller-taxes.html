Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) Maximization

Understanding COGS for Etsy Sellers

Cost of Goods Sold is one of the most powerful deductions for product-based businesses. COGS includes all direct costs of creating your products, and it's deducted from your gross income before calculating business profit and taxes.

COGS Category What's Included Tax Impact
Raw Materials Fabric, yarn, beads, metal, wood, clay, paint Direct reduction of taxable income
Components & Parts Findings, hardware, electronics, packaging inserts Direct reduction of taxable income
Direct Labor Your time creating products (if incorporated) Reduces business income
Manufacturing Overhead Portion of utilities, studio rent for production Allocated to COGS
Inventory Purchases Finished goods purchased for resale Deducted when sold

COGS Calculation Methods

Etsy sellers can choose from several inventory accounting methods:

FIFO (First In, First Out)

  • Method: Oldest inventory costs are expensed first
  • Best for: Rising material costs (common scenario)
  • Tax effect: Lower COGS, higher taxable income during inflation

LIFO (Last In, First Out)

  • Method: Newest inventory costs are expensed first
  • Best for: Falling material costs (less common)
  • Tax effect: Higher COGS, lower taxable income during inflation

Average Cost Method

  • Method: Average cost of all inventory items
  • Best for: Simplified tracking, minimal price fluctuations
  • Tax effect: Smooths out cost variations

‚ö†Ô∏è COGS vs. Business Expense Classification

Materials that become part of your final product are COGS. Tools, equipment, and supplies used to create products but don't become part of them are business expenses. For example: yarn used in a scarf is COGS, the knitting needles are business expenses.

Inventory Management and Tax Strategies

Beginning and Ending Inventory

Proper inventory tracking is crucial for accurate COGS calculations:

  • Beginning inventory: Value of materials/products on hand at start of tax year
  • Purchases: Materials and inventory bought during the year
  • Ending inventory: Value of materials/products remaining at year end
  • COGS formula: Beginning Inventory + Purchases - Ending Inventory = COGS

Inventory Valuation Methods

You must consistently value your inventory using one of these methods:

Valuation Method How It Works Best For
Cost Method Value at actual purchase price Most small businesses, simple tracking
Lower of Cost or Market Value at cost or current market value (whichever is lower) Businesses with fluctuating material costs
Retail Method Based on retail selling price minus markup Businesses with consistent markup percentages

üìä Year-End Inventory Strategy

Consider timing inventory purchases and production near year-end. Buying materials in December increases your deductible expenses, while finishing products in January keeps them out of current-year COGS calculations.

Sales Tax Collection and Remittance

Understanding Sales Tax Nexus

Since the 2018 Supreme Court Wayfair decision, many states require online sellers to collect sales tax based on economic nexus thresholds:

Nexus Type Requirement Typical Thresholds
Physical Nexus Physical presence in state Any physical presence
Economic Nexus Sales volume or transaction count $100,000 or 200 transactions
Click-Through Nexus Affiliate marketing in state Varies by state
Marketplace Nexus Sales through marketplace facilitators Platform handles collection

Sales Tax Registration and Collection

When you meet nexus thresholds, you must:

  • Register for sales tax permits in each state where you have nexus
  • Collect appropriate tax rates based on customer location
  • File regular returns (monthly, quarterly, or annually)
  • Remit collected taxes by required deadlines

‚ö†Ô∏è Etsy's Tax Collection Service

Etsy automatically collects sales tax for many states, but you're still responsible for registration, filing returns, and remitting taxes in some jurisdictions. Don't assume Etsy handles everything ‚Äì verify your obligations in each state.
